DESCRIPTION

Overview

- Sparkling wine is usually white or rosé, but red sparkling wines are also available.
- Sweetness Sparkling wines can range from very dry to sweet, with sweetness levels indicated on the label. For example, Brut nature is bone dry, while Doux is very sweet.
- Pairing Sparkling wines are light and refreshing, so they pair well with heavy, fatty foods.

Alcohol Conatins

- Alcohol by volume (ABV): Sparkling wine typically has an ABV of around 10.5–12.5%. However, the ABV can vary depending on the type of sparkling wine:-
- California Sparkling Wine: ABV of 12.5–13.5%
- French Champagne: ABV of 12.5–13.5%
- Spanish Cava: ABV of 12.5–13.5%
- Sekt: ABV of 12.5–13.5%
- Italian Asti: ABV of around 7.5%

Wine Taste

- The sparkling wine is made with red wine, white wine or a combination of both, it can vary in sweetness, dryness, flavor and mouthfeel.
- Vintage Champagnes are known for their nutty, toasty notes and tiny bubbles, while Prosecco leans more fruit forward with more aggressive carbonation.

Benefits

- Heart health Sparkling wine can improve heart health because it contains antioxidants and anti-inflammatory compounds from red grapes. It also contains traces of copper, which can help prevent high blood pressure and high cholesterol.
- Gut health The polyphenols in sparkling wine can promote the growth of good gut bacteria.
- Skin health The carbon dioxide in sparkling wine can help reduce wrinkles and stretch marks, and increase skin elasticity.
Brain health Sparkling wine can improve brain health, including memory, attention span, and spatial awareness.

Capacity Of Bottle

- A standard 750 milliliter (mL) bottle of sparkling wine, like Champagne or Prosecco, typically contains enough wine for six glasses.
- This is based on a standard serving size of 125 mL or 4 fluid ounces per glass
