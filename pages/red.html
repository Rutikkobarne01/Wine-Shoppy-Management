Wine Taste

- Color: A ruby red color may indicate a younger wine with vibrant red fruit flavors. An aged red wine will change to garnet and may have a brick-red rim.
- Clarity: A hazy appearance with hints of brown may indicate it's corked.
- Density: A deep and dense color indicates a full-bodied style with a richer mouthfeel, bolder flavors, and higher in alcohol.
- Smell Inhale deeply and try to identify the different aromas. Red wine can have primary aromas of fruit and berry, secondary aromas of yeasty, and tertiary aromas of vanilla, nuts, spices, woods, toasted breads, leathery smells, smoked or burnt smells, and butter.
- Taste Take a small sip and allow it to linger on your palate.
