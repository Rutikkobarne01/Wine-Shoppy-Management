DESCRIPTION

Overview

- Merlot is a dark blue–colored wine grape variety that is used as both a blending grape and for varietal wines.
-The name Merlot is thought to be a diminutive of merle, the French name for the blackbird, probably a reference to the color of the grape.

Alcohol Conatins

- Merlot wines typically contain 13.5–14.5% alcohol by volume (ABV), which is relatively high compared to other wines.
- However, the exact alcohol content can vary depending on the region and specific wine.
- For example, Merlots grown in cooler climates, like France, usually have an ABV of 13–14%, while those grown in warmer climates, like Australia, California, and Chile, can have an ABV of 14.5%

Wine Taste

- Merlot is a medium- to full-bodied wine with notes of red fruit, like cherry, plum, and raspberry.
- It also has notes of oak, graphite, black cherries, and cocoa.
-When aged in oak, it can also have hints of cedar, vanilla, and clove

Benefits

- Merlot wines are often described as being "smooth" and "velvety." Merlot is also a rich source of resveratrol, which can help your cardiovascular health.
-Studies have found that Merlot contains high levels of procyanidin, a compound that helps to prevent cholesterol from buildup in the arteries
