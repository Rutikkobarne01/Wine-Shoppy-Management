DESCRIPTION

Overview

- Rosé is made by briefly soaking crushed grapes in their skins, which can range from a few hours to a couple of days.
- The skins are then removed, and the juice is fermented like a white wine

Alcohol Conatins

- The alcohol content of rosé wine can vary, but it's usually around 11–14% ABV. Some rosés can have an ABV as high as 14.5%.
- For example, Tavel rosé is known for being higher in alcohol content, usually with a minimum of 11%.

Wine Taste

- The primary flavors of rosé wine are red fruit, flowers, citrus, and melon, with a pleasant crunchy green flavor on the finish similar to celery or rhubarb.

Benefits

- It contains antioxidants that can help reduce inflammation and lower the risk of heart disease.
- Additionally, it contains resveratrol, which has been linked to improved brain function and a reduced risk of Alzheimer's disease.

Capacity Of Bottle
