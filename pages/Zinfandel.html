DESCRIPTION

Overview

- The primary flavors of Zinfandel are jam, blueberry, black pepper, cherry, plum, boysenberry, cranberry, and licorice.
- When you taste Zinfandel it often explodes with candied fruitiness followed by spice and often a tobacco-like smoky finish

Alcohol Conatins

- Generally speaking, most Zinfandel wines have higher alcohol levels ranging from about 14 – 17% ABV.
- Higher alcohol adds an oily texture and bigger, bolder body.

Wine Taste

- Zinfandel wine, you can consider its: Color: Zinfandel is a deep, layered red wine that can range in color from pink to deep purple.
- Smell: Zinfandel has aromas of blackberry, anise, and pepper.
- Taste: Zinfandel is a rich, fruit-forward wine with flavors that vary depending on how ripe the grapes
- Early picked: Hints of blackberry, black cherry, plums, and red apple Later picked: Strawberry, cranberry, and raspberry notes
- Alcohol by Volume (ABV): A lighter Zinfandel will have about 13.5% ABV, while a bold and spicy Zinfandel will have around 16% ABV
- Tannins: High quality Zinfandel will have high tannin
- Clarity: Wines that display a murky or cloudy appearance are not suitable for consumption

Benefits

- Zinfandel is great for your heart. More specifically, the black grapes offer protection against heart attacks and vascular disease.
- Other benefits are better eyesight due to increased levels of carotenoids along with helping brain functions and reducing migraines.2
