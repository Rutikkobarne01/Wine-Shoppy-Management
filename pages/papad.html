Overview

- A "masala papad" is a popular Indian snack consisting of a crispy papad (a thin, flatbread made from lentil flour)
- Topped with a mixture of chopped onions, tomatoes, green chilies, coriander leaves, and a blend of spices
- like chaat masala, red chili powder, and lemon juice, essentially creating a flavorful, crunchy appetizer or side dish; often served at restaurants as a starter.

Taste of Masala Papad

- Papad is nothing but the thin Indian wafer, which can be refereed as a cracker or flat bread.
- Papad is generally made from dried lentils, it can be eaten fried or roasted.

- it's essentially a flavorful, quick snack with a mix of textures and tastes, perfect for sharing and often garnished with sev (crispy noodles) for added crunch.
